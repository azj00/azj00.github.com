Elephant Edu

Spring, 2022

Overview

This project is my Keystone Project for Kilachand Honors College (Boston University). This project helps showcase my problem-solving skill, design choice and prototyping skill. I used Lucidchart for ideation and Figma for Hi-fi prototyping.

Proposal

BU students who do not often work out may be confused about the proper way to use the gym equipment at the BU Fitness and Recreation Center(FitRec). Even in gym, machines made by different brands may have different usage instructions. Furthermore, international students often find the facilities hard to use because they have trouble reading the instructions. For example, they may not know that the treadmills are set to miles by default. Miles are longer than kilometers, and so a common and dangerous mistake is to set the treadmill to a particular speed thinking that it is in kilometers per hour only to find that the speed is much faster than expected. To compound the issue, students might be too shy to ask for help or may not know the gym rules.

Survey Analysis

1. Secondary Research: How do students learn to use a workout machine?

  • YouTube Videos
  • Reddit
  • Personal Trainer App (i.e Keep, CoPilot, EverFit, etc)
  • Ask other students in the gym
  • Personal Trainer
  • Instructions on the machine

2. Survey

2.1 Survey Goal

If an international (Chinese) BU student has trouble learning how to use BU Fitrec Facilities

2.2 Survey Target Group
  • International student
  • Have worked out in Fitrec before the interview
  • Use Fitrec Facilities/ machines to workout
  • Age above 18
2.3 Survey Results
  • There are 5 survey responses and 2 interviewees
  • Students who have a lot of workout experience don’t usually have problems understanding how a machine works because they already know how to use them.
  • There are some students who complain that it’s hard to find when the swimming pool is available because they don’t know if there are professional swimmers who need to practice.
  • All students who have been to Fitrec uses machines to workout
  • Most students learn to use a new machine through observing others. They also learn to use a new machine from a professional trainer, YouTube videos or instructions on the machine.
  • Most participants feel embarrassed when trying a new machine and that stops them from trying. None of them have trouble reading English instructions.
  • Most participants feel most comfortable trying a new machine with a guide, either through YouTube videos, friends, or personal trainer.
  • Most participants didn’t try the recreation facilities because they don’t have friends to go with.
  • Most participants agree that having easy detailed access to instructions will encourage students to try new machines.

Pain Points

  • Financial: Students may not have the money to hire a personal trainer to teach them how to use the machines. Personal training at Fitrec is expensive. It is also not clear what one would learn in an hour-long training session. In addition, they are stuck with working out in FitRec because they do not have the money to go to other gyms that have better service and/or equipment.
  • Product: There is no start button for elliptical crosstrainer. Students might be confused about how to start it.
  • Process: Students can't find if the equipment or recreation spaces are full. Students cannot know beforehand if a space is reserved by a club or professional training.
  • Support: There are no workers to demonstrate how a machine works. The instructions on the machine are not clear enough. For students who reserved a space, there is no gym employee to enforce the reservation.
